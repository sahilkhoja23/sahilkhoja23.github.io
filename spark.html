Spark

iOS Application · 24 hours

Overview

Spark was conceived and designed during the Money 20/20 Hackathon in 24 hours with Mathew Black, Sameer Khoja, and Aalyan Rajwani. The idea was based on the Ice Bucket Challenge, except you could design any challenge and and proceeds would go towards a cause that you cared about. The purpose was to reduce the friction that people come across when trying to donate to a cause, since many people don't know the organizations/non-profits/NGO's associated with the cause. We wanted to create a fun way to leverage the virality of the ALS Ice Bucket Challenge to support all organizations alike.

Role

Implementation

There were a ton of changes, especially to the user interface, within 24 hours. First, the causes page. I had initially used emojis to describe causes, but after testing the page with users, many people were confused as to what each emoji represented. Our team fixed the issue by developing custom icons to represent the causes that people could support. Another central issue was crating a 'Spark'. Each Spark had 4 parameters: the challenge, duration of the challenge, the amount one would have to give if they didn't complete the challenge, and the cause. My initial design was very similar to composing an email, as an email has a similar amount of parameters. But it didn't seem interactive, or fun. Many people associate email with work, and we definitely didn't want our app to seem like work. Especially since you're being requesting to give money.
